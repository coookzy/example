Who's Gonna Carry The Boats? (Not You, You're Too Soft)

Navy SEAL training. Hell Week. Twenty boats, 200-pound logs, surf torture, sleep deprivation. At some point, someone has to carry the boat. Not hold it. Carry it. All the way. Through the surf. Up the beach. Up the hill. In the dark. When every muscle is screaming and every rational thought is telling you to put it down.

Someone always does it. Not everyone. Someone.

In this space, right now, someone is carrying the boat. They're building through the bear market. They're shipping while everyone else is posting about shipping. They're the one whose commit history doesn't have a six-month gap where "the vibes were off."

The Boat Is Heavy. That's the Point.

The boat is heavy because easy things don't test anything. Building on a convenient timeline when price action is good and the community is excited is not carrying the boat. That's being carried by the boat. The boat carries you when conditions are favorable.

Carrying the boat means building when the price is down. Shipping when nobody is watching. Maintaining the protocol when the governance forum has three active participants. Reviewing PRs when you're tired. Answering the support question at 11pm because you said you would.

That's the boat. And most people put it down.

What Happens When People Put the Boat Down

The project stalls. The community loses confidence. The small issues become big issues because nobody addressed them when they were small. The developers who could have shipped it pivot to the next thing. The early believers feel betrayed. The technology — which might have been genuinely good — dies not because it was bad but because nobody carried it far enough.

This has happened to hundreds of projects in this space. It will happen to hundreds more. Not because the tech was wrong. Because the people put the boat down when carrying it got hard.

How to Know If You're Carrying the Boat

You're carrying the boat if you're working on the thing when nobody is watching. If you'd still be building even if the token never recovered. If you can describe specifically what you shipped this week without looking at Jira. If your answer to "what are you working on" is a deployed URL, not a concept.

You're NOT carrying the boat if your productivity is correlated with the price chart. If you need external validation to keep going. If "difficult market conditions" is your explanation for why nothing shipped in three months.
